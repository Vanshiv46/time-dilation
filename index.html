01 — Concept

What is
Time Dilation?

Time dilation is the slowing of time experienced by a moving observer relative to a stationary one. It is not an illusion — clocks genuinely tick at different rates depending on relative velocity.

Einstein's 1905 Special Relativity is built on two foundational postulates:

I
The laws of physics are identical in all inertial (non-accelerating) frames of reference.
II
The speed of light in a vacuum is constant at 299,792,458 m/s for all observers — regardless of the source's motion.
Light Clock — why moving clocks tick slower
02 — Mathematics

The Formula

The exact relationship between time in a moving frame and a rest frame — derived purely from the two postulates above.

t' = t / √( 1 − v² / c² )
equivalently → t' = γt   where   γ = 1/√(1−β²)   and   β = v/c
variables defined
t'
Dilated time
Time in the moving frame. Always greater than t — the astronaut ages slower.
t
Proper time
Time in the rest frame. What an Earth-based clock reads.
v
Relative velocity
Speed of the moving object relative to the observer. Must be less than c.
c
Speed of light
≈ 3×10⁸ m/s. The universal speed limit. Nothing with mass can reach it.
γ
Lorentz factor
Always ≥ 1. Equals 1 at rest, approaches ∞ as v→c.
β
Beta (v/c)
Velocity as a fraction of c. Ranges 0 (rest) to just under 1 (near-light).

04 — Real World

Where It
Actually Happens

🛰️
GPS Satellites
GPS satellites orbit at ~14,000 km/h. Their clocks run slow by ~7 µs/day. Without relativity corrections, GPS navigation would drift by 2+ km every single day.
γ ≈ 1.000000001
⚛️
Cosmic Muons
Muons from cosmic rays travel at 0.998c. Half-life of 2.2 µs means they should decay before reaching Earth. Time dilation stretches their lifetime by γ≈15 — they do reach us.
γ ≈ 15.8
✈️
Hafele-Keating
1971: Atomic clocks flown on commercial jets were compared to ground clocks. The flying clocks lost exactly the nanoseconds predicted by Special Relativity. First direct experimental proof.
~59 ns lost
🎬
Interstellar (2014)
On Miller's planet near black hole Gargantua: 1 hour = 7 Earth years. Vetted by Nobel physicist Kip Thorne — the most scientifically accurate cinematic depiction of time dilation.
1 hr = 7 yrs
🔬
CERN Accelerator
Particles at CERN reach 0.9999c with γ up to 7,000. Unstable particles survive thousands of times longer in the lab frame. Engineers account for this in every design.
γ up to 7,000
🌑
Black Hole Edge
Near an event horizon, gravitational time dilation (General Relativity) becomes extreme. For a distant observer, a clock dropped into a black hole appears to freeze permanently.
γ → ∞
05 — History

How We
Got Here

From a failed experiment in 1887 to atomic clocks on jets — the century that proved time is not what we thought.

1887
Michelson-Morley Experiment
Failed to detect the "luminiferous ether." Showed light speed is the same in all directions. The crisis that made relativity necessary.
1905
Einstein's Annus Mirabilis
Published "On the Electrodynamics of Moving Bodies" — introducing Special Relativity and the time dilation equation to the world.
1908
Minkowski Spacetime
Hermann Minkowski unified space and time into a 4D manifold. "Henceforth, space by itself and time by itself are doomed to fade away."
1938
Ives-Stilwell Experiment
First direct experimental confirmation of time dilation using the transverse Doppler effect on fast-moving hydrogen canal rays.
1971
Hafele-Keating Experiment
Atomic clocks on jets matched relativistic predictions exactly. Time dilation is real, measurable, and now used in everyday technology.
2002
Penning Trap Measurement
Relativistic time dilation confirmed to 2.2 parts in 10²² — the most precise verification of Special Relativity ever achieved.
06 — Thought Experiment

The Twin
Paradox

Arjun stays on Earth. Priya boards a rocket to a star 10 light-years away at 0.87c and returns.

On return: Arjun has aged 23 years. Priya has aged only 11.5 years. She is now younger than her twin.

The apparent paradox: both see the other as moving — so shouldn't both age less? No. Priya physically decelerates and turns around. She experiences a real force. Arjun never does. This breaks the symmetry.

Resolution
Special Relativity applies only to inertial (non-accelerating) frames. Priya's turnaround involves real acceleration — she feels it. Arjun never accelerates. This asymmetry is what determines who ages less. No paradox exists.
