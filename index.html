01 — Concept

What is Time Dilation?

Time dilation is a difference in elapsed time measured by two observers — one at rest and one moving relative to each other, or at different gravitational potentials.

Proposed by Albert Einstein in 1905, it is a direct consequence of the two postulates of Special Relativity: that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames, and that the speed of light is constant for all observers.

Simply put: the faster you move, the slower your clock ticks relative to a stationary observer.

02 — Mathematics

The Formula

The exact mathematical relationship between time in a moving frame versus a rest frame.

t' = t / √(1 − v²/c²)
or equivalently:   t' = γ · t   where   γ = 1/√(1 − β²)
t'
Dilated Time
Time elapsed in the moving frame (what the astronaut experiences)
t
Proper Time
Time elapsed in the rest frame (what the Earth observer measures)
v
Relative Velocity
Speed of the moving object relative to the observer
c
Speed of Light
≈ 3 × 10⁸ m/s — the universal speed limit
γ
Lorentz Factor
Always ≥ 1. Approaches ∞ as v → c. This is the "stretching" factor.
β
Beta (v/c)
Velocity as a fraction of the speed of light. β=0 at rest, β→1 at light speed.

04 — Real World

Where Time Dilation
Actually Happens

🛰️
GPS Satellites
GPS satellites orbit at ~14,000 km/h. Special relativity causes their clocks to lose ~7 microseconds/day. Without correction, your GPS would drift by 2+ km every day!
γ ≈ 1.000000001
⚛️
Cosmic Ray Muons
Muons created in the upper atmosphere travel at 0.998c. Their half-life is 2.2 µs — they should decay before reaching us. But time dilation stretches their lifespan and they survive to the surface.
γ ≈ 15.8
✈️
Hafele-Keating (1971)
Atomic clocks were flown around the world on commercial jets and compared to ground clocks. The flying clocks lost exactly the nanoseconds predicted by Special Relativity — first direct proof!
~59 nanoseconds lost
🎬
Interstellar (2014)
Near the black hole Gargantua, Cooper's team experience 1 hour while 7 years pass on Earth. The film's science was vetted by physicist Kip Thorne — an accurate portrayal of extreme time dilation.
1 hour = 7 years
🔬
Particle Accelerators
At CERN, particles accelerated to 0.9999c experience time dilation of γ ≈ 7000. Engineers must account for this in their designs — unstable particles live thousands of times longer in the lab frame.
γ up to 7,000
🌟
Near Black Holes
Gravitational time dilation (General Relativity) causes clocks near massive objects to tick slower. At the event horizon of a black hole, time effectively stops for an outside observer.
γ → ∞ at event horizon
05 — Famous Thought Experiment

The Twin Paradox

Imagine two twins: Alex stays on Earth, while Sam travels to a distant star at 0.87c and returns.

When Sam returns, Alex has aged 20 years — but Sam has only aged 10 years. Sam is now 10 years younger than their twin!

The "paradox": shouldn't each twin see the other as moving? Yes — but Sam actually accelerates (turns around), breaking the symmetry. The one who accelerates ages less. This is NOT a paradox — Special Relativity resolves it completely.

Key Insight
The resolution: inertial symmetry is broken when Sam decelerates, turns around, and accelerates back. The asymmetry in acceleration determines who ages less.
